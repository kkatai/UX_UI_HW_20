DEPARTMENT OF INTERIOR REDESIGN

Here's my process of redesigning the DOI's website

Overview

The problem on the website was that the navigation was confusing, pictures weren’t relevant to the content, and lack of representation on the Tribal Nations page.

The solution was to redesign the Department of Interior’s desktop and mobile site, focusing on main and footer navigation issues to the Tribal Nations Page.

User Research

I worked with a partner for the user research portion of the project, to stay in scope and brainstorm ideas. We started the research process by researching, investigating & annotating the website and conducting a heuristics evaluation to better understand who and why our user would visit the site.

We conducted 5 usability tests to get a better idea of what people's pain points in navigating the site would be.

The main frustrations were:

  • layout/design was confusing,
  • users didn't know what the 'Priority Tab' was,
  • the graphics felt irrelevant, and
  • people generally felt the site was overall not user-friendly

From this information, we created the persona Winona, who would be trying to find resources on the Tribal Nations page within the site, which we demonstrated through user flow.

Definition & Synthesis

We conducted multiple rounds of card sorting to try to recategorize the information within the page to create a more intuitive navigating experience. A big change that notibly needed to be changed was the nav bar categories.

Ideation

From this point, I began to branch from my research partner and began to sketch out a new layout for the site. After wrestling with different categories for the nav bar, I conducted some usability tests to see if the new navigation system made sense to users.

Prototyping Lo-fi to Hi-fi

I condensed the amount of physical tabs and simplified the information on the homepage to try to make it more clean and user-friendly. Once I got feedback that the navigation felt intuitive and straightforward on the lo-fi wireframes, I applied a nature-inspired color scheme (that also matched their logo) in order to add clarity and visual inspiration for the users.

User Testing & Outcomes

I went through a LOT of rounds of iterations on the original 'Priority Tab' naming, eventually settling on just naming it what was inside: 'Bureaus, Offices, & Services'. It was probably the biggest pain point for users on understanding what was there. It made more sense to expand the title instead of adding 3 tabs to the navigation system for simplicity. Overall, I wanted the site to feel updated and earth, while not being too modern, as to be accessible for users of all ages.

Conclusions & Future Opportunities

In future, I'd love to expand upon more pages instead of just Tribal Nations, but in the beginning I felt it was one of the most confusing pages to navigate through, which is why I chose to iterate on it. I chose to stick with the one flow to keep my scope tight for the time I had to complete the project. I found that laying out the navigation/basics first makes it easier for everything else to fall into place in the layout, but because people were having problems clicking through the site to begin with, I made the navigation system my top priority in redesigning. While I like the chosen color scheme, I do feel like the site is a bit bright, and would probably go back and iterate on some of the shades to keep them warm but a little more toned down, or maybe switch out the hero image. I'd also make the hero scrollable or add actual content to more of the interior pages instead of having a generic "catchall" page that I kept for time's sake.
